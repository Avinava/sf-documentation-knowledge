# Provide Free Trials on Your Website

Use HTML forms to drive traffic to your business and show off your solutions to prospective customers. After a prospect submits your form, Salesforce provisions a trial based on your Trialforce template.

To provide a free trial on your website, first [set up Trialforce](atlas.en-us.packagingGuide.meta/packagingGuide/trialforce_setup_overview.htm#trialforce_setup_overview "Use Trialforce to deliver free trials of your AppExchange solution in Salesforce orgs that customers can keep and customize. Learn Trialforce key concepts, relationships, and best practices. Then set up Trialforce, create a Trialforce template for your solution, and add it to your AppExchange listing."). Then, complete the following tasks and you’re ready to go live.

-   **[Enable the SignupRequest API](atlas.en-us.packagingGuide.meta/packagingGuide/trialforce_enable_signuprequest_api.htm)**
    To enable the SignupRequest API in your Salesforce org, log a case.
-   **[Choose a Sign-Up Form Hosting Option](atlas.en-us.packagingGuide.meta/packagingGuide/trialforce_signup_form_hosting.htm)**
    The sign-up form serves as the registration page that prospective customers use to sign up for trials. Review and choose a hosting option for your sign-up form.
-   **[Sign-Ups Using the API](atlas.en-us.packagingGuide.meta/packagingGuide/trialforce_signup_api_introduction.htm)**
    Use API calls to the SignupRequest object to create sign-ups for prospective customers.
-   **[Create Proxy Signups for OAuth and API Access](atlas.en-us.packagingGuide.meta/packagingGuide/trialforce_proxy_signup.htm)**
    Using the SignupRequest object, you can programmatically create an org without any system-generated emails being sent to the user.
-   **[Provision Trial Orgs](atlas.en-us.packagingGuide.meta/packagingGuide/trialforce_provision_trial_orgs.htm)**
    Use Trialforce to provision a free trial of your solution for prospective customers.
